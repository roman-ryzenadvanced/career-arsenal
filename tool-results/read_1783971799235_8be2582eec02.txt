    19→## The Problem
    20→
    21→Career management is a **full-time job** — for individuals AND the HR teams who support them. Job seekers struggle with researching companies, tailoring resumes, and negotiating offers. HR teams wrestle with sourcing candidates, running structured interviews, building pipelines, and retaining top talent. Most people do none of it well because they're too busy working their actual job.
    22→
    23→## The Solution
    24→
    25→**Career Arsenal** is a collection of **16 AI-powered skills** organized into two sets:
    26→
    27→- **Career Skills** (8 skills) — For job seekers and career builders: from deciding whether to switch jobs, to landing the offer, to negotiating compensation.
    28→- **HR Team Skills** (8 skills) — For HR managers and hiring teams: from agentic candidate sourcing on LinkedIn, to structured interviews, to retention analytics.

   114→## Skill Details
   115→
   116→### 1. JobHunter Master
   117→**The aggressive job hunting skill that goes beyond Easy Apply.**
   118→
   119→6-phase workflow: Target Acquisition → Intelligence Gathering → Material Customization → Multi-Channel Approach → Follow-Up Cadence → Track & Iterate. Includes templates for LinkedIn connection requests, cold emails, and follow-ups. Integrates with browser, bird (Twitter), gog (Gmail), and nano-pdf skills.
   120→
   121→### 2. Resume Architect
   122→**One resume does not fit all. Build a tailored resume for every application.**
   123→
   124→Reverse-engineers job posts into keyword fingerprints, then assembles ATS-compliant resumes that match. Supports entry-level, mid-level, senior/staff templates, and a career-changer mode. Outputs Markdown, LaTeX, PDF, or JSON.
   125→
   126→### 3. Cover Letter Craft
   127→**Write letters that get read — not recycled.**
   128→
   129→4-paragraph formula: Hook → Proof → Value → Close. Tone-calibrated for startups, scale-ups, enterprise, and agencies. Includes templates for engineers, career changers, and senior/leadership roles. Anti-patterns section keeps you from common mistakes.
   130→
   131→### 4. Interview Commander
   132→**Be the most prepared candidate they've ever interviewed.**
   133→
   134→Builds a story arsenal (10 STAR-L stories covering all question types), generates role-specific question banks, provides reverse-interview questions, and includes a mock interview mode where Claude acts as the interviewer. Covers technical screens, behavioral rounds, panels, and case studies. Post-interview: thank-you templates and self-assessment scorecards.
   135→
   136→### 5. Salary Negotiator
   137→**Know your worth. Anchor high. Never accept the first offer.**
   138→
   139→Market research across levels.fyi, Glassdoor, Blind, and LinkedIn. Total compensation breakdown (base, equity, bonus, sign-on, benefits, title). Negotiation scripts for phone, email, and in-person. Handles common pushback ("above our range", "budget is fixed"). Includes startup-specific equity negotiation and offer review checklist.
   140→
   141→### 6. Career GPS
   142→**Don't drift through your career. Navigate it with intent.**
   143→
   144→Full career audit (technical, leadership, business skills). Gap analysis with priority matrix. Quarterly action roadmaps. Transition playbooks: IC to Manager, Backend to Full Stack, Big Tech to Startup, Dev to PM, Employee to Freelancer. Quarterly review template to keep yourself accountable.
   145→
   146→### 7. LinkedIn Optimizer
   147→**Turn your profile into a recruiter magnet.**
   148→
   149→Profile audit scoring (10 dimensions). Headline engineering for LinkedIn SEO. About section writing with keyword density. Experience section optimization with achievement-focused bullets. Content strategy for algorithmic reach. Network growth playbook with connection templates.
   150→
   151→### 8. Job Switch Advisor
   152→**Decide whether to stay, switch, or pivot — with data, not emotions.**
   153→
   154→Stay/Switch diagnostic (20-point scoring). Problem diagnosis (can this be fixed without leaving?). Market reality check. Switch cost analysis (equity, bonus, benefits, relationships). Decision matrix. Includes both a "Stay Strategy" (90-day improvement plan) and a "Switch Strategy" (12-week search timeline). Resignation template included.

   158→## HR Team Skill Details
   159→
   160→### 9. HR Candidate Hunter
   161→**Agentic candidate sourcing that finds people who aren't looking.**
   162→
   163→6-phase workflow: Ideal Candidate Profile → Agentic LinkedIn Sourcing (Boolean mastery, 50-100 profiles/day) → Multi-Platform Sourcing (GitHub, Twitter/X, Stack Overflow, communities) → Outreach Engine (personalized templates, follow-up cadence, 25-40% reply rates) → Screening & Qualification (15-min phone screen script, green/red flags) → Pipeline Building (hot/warm/cold/nurture tiers, monthly nurture touchpoints). Includes AI-assisted candidate scoring rubric and automated daily sourcing loop.
   164→
   165→### 10. HR Job Description Forge
   166→**Write JDs that attract top talent — not filter it out.**
   167→
   168→5-phase workflow: Role Definition (what will they accomplish in 90 days?) → JD Structure (8-part template tested across 10K+ postings) → Inclusive Language Audit (gender-coded words, age signals, unnecessary degree requirements) → SEO & Distribution (10+ channels, keyword optimization) → A/B Testing JDs. Includes engineering and non-technical role templates. Anti-patterns section keeps you from common mistakes.
   169→
   170→### 11. HR Interview Designer
   171→**Structured interviews that predict performance and eliminate bias.**
   172→
   173→5-phase workflow: Competency Mapping (JD requirements → interviewable competencies with 1-5 rubrics) → Interview Architecture (round structure, timing, panel composition) → Question Bank (system design, coding, behavioral STAR, leadership, reverse interview) → Rubric Design (5-point scored rubrics per question with evidence requirements) → Debrief Framework (simultaneous score sharing, anti-bias rules, decision framework). Candidate experience checklist included.
   174→
   175→### 12. HR Offer Architect
   176→**Design compelling offers that close top candidates without overpaying.**
   177→
   178→5-phase workflow: Compensation Benchmarking (P25-P90 across 7 data sources) → Offer Structure (base, bonus, equity, sign-on, benefits, title — every component with negotiation leverage) → Offer Presentation (verbal-first delivery, written within 24 hours, personal touches) → Handling Negotiations (scripts for: more base, competing offers, more equity, different title, remote requests) → Closing Best Practices. Includes startup-specific equity communication guide.
   179→
   180→### 13. HR Onboarding Commander
   181→**Turn new hires into productive, engaged team members from Day 1.**
   182→
   183→5-phase workflow: Pre-boarding (logistics, people, communication — everything ready before Day 1) → Week 1 (day-by-day schedule: welcome, setup, first task, team introductions) → Week 2-4 (ramp: first feature, codebase walkthrough, first month check-in) → Month 2-3 (ownership, cross-functional relationships, Day 90 review with 6 structured questions) → Remote Adaptations (over-invest in communication, documentation, inclusion). Warning sign detection at every stage.
   184→
   185→### 14. HR Retention Radar
   186→**Predict attrition risk, diagnose causes, and retain your best people.**
   187→
   188→5-phase workflow: Flight Risk Detection (14-signal scorecard, quarterly scoring) → Stay Interview Framework (10 questions, conducted by HR/skip-level, not direct manager) → Root Cause Diagnosis (5 categories: manager issues, growth stagnation, comp disconnect, burnout, culture misalignment — with targeted fixes for each) → Retention Playbooks (top performers, new hires, tenured employees, team-wide disengagement) → Retention Infrastructure (comp, growth, recognition, management quality, culture systems). Exit interview framework included.
   189→
   190→### 15. HR Culture Architect
   191→**Build, measure, and evolve a culture that attracts great people.**
   192→
   193→5-phase workflow: Culture Audit (10-dimension scoring: trust, autonomy, growth, inclusion, transparency, accountability, collaboration, recognition, wellbeing, purpose) → Values Definition (specific, testable, sometimes controversial — with behavioral examples and hire/fire criteria) → Culture Rituals (daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, annual — with anti-rituals to eliminate) → Culture at Scale (inflection points: 1→10, 10→50, 50→200, 200→1000, 1000+ with specific actions at each stage) → Measuring Culture Health (eNPS, attrition rate, engagement scores, qualitative themes).
   194→
   195→### 16. HR Talent Pipeline Builder
   196→**Build a continuous talent engine — not reactive, one-off recruiting.**
   197→
   198→5-phase workflow: Hiring Forecast (rolling 12-month plan by quarter with priority, timeline, and source strategy) → Employer Brand Engine (career page audit, LinkedIn/engineering blog/Twitter content strategy, Glassdoor management, events) → Pipeline Categories (4 tiers: active candidates, warm leads, passive prospects, future talent with CRM structure) → Nurture Programs (monthly touchpoints, quarterly events, referral engine, university pipeline) → Pipeline Metrics (funnel conversion rates, coverage ratios, time-to-fill, cost-per-hire, source quality). Diversity & inclusion pipeline section included.

   403→### OpenCode (sst/opencode)
   404→
   405→OpenCode loads custom agent instructions from `AGENTS.md` in the project root. The `build` and `plan` agents pick up these instructions automatically.
   406→

   597→5. **Decision Matrix** — Compare Stay vs. Switch across salary, title, growth, happiness, risk. Includes Stay Strategy (90-day plan) and Switch Strategy (12-week timeline)

   802→### Hermes Agent
   803→
   804→Hermes Agent supports custom skill files that can be loaded into agent sessions.
   805→
   806→```bash
   807→# Clone the repository
   808→git clone https://github.rommark.dev/admin/Job-Hunter-Linkedin-Skill-Hermes-OpenClaw-ClaudeCode-Comet.git /tmp/career-arsenal
   809→
   810→# Copy all 16 skills to your Hermes configuration directory
   811→mkdir -p ~/.hermes/skills
   812→cp /tmp/career-arsenal/skills/*.md ~/.hermes/skills/
   813→
   814→# Reference skills in your Hermes agent config
   815→# Add to your agent configuration file:
   816→#
   817→# skills:
   818→#   # Career Skills
   819→#   - path: ~/.hermes/skills/jobhunter-master.md
   820→#   - path: ~/.hermes/skills/resume-architect.md
   821→#   - path: ~/.hermes/skills/cover-letter-craft.md
   822→#   - path: ~/.hermes/skills/interview-commander.md
   823→#   - path: ~/.hermes/skills/salary-negotiator.md
   824→#   - path: ~/.hermes/skills/career-gps.md
   825→#   - path: ~/.hermes/skills/linkedin-optimizer.md
   826→#   - path: ~/.hermes/skills/job-switch-advisor.md
   827→#   # HR Team Skills
   828→#   - path: ~/.hermes/skills/hr-candidate-hunter.md
   829→#   - path: ~/.hermes/skills/hr-job-description-forge.md
   830→#   - path: ~/.hermes/skills/hr-interview-designer.md
   831→#   - path: ~/.hermes/skills/hr-offer-architect.md
   832→#   - path: ~/.hermes/skills/hr-onboarding-commander.md
   833→#   - path: ~/.hermes/skills/hr-retention-radar.md
   834→#   - path: ~/.hermes/skills/hr-culture-architect.md
   835→#   - path: ~/.hermes/skills/hr-talent-pipeline.md
   836→```
   837→
   838→Or load a skill inline when starting a Hermes session:
   839→```bash
   840→# Load a specific skill into context
   841→hermes --skill ~/.hermes/skills/hr-candidate-hunter.md
   842→
   843→# Load all skills
   844→hermes --skills-dir ~/.hermes/skills/
   845→```
   846→
   847→Once loaded, reference skills by name in your conversation. Hermes will apply the full workflow when it detects career or HR-related requests.

  1000→1. Fork the repository
  1001→2. Create a feature branch (`git checkout -b skill/your-skill-name`)
  1002→3. Follow the existing skill format (Philosophy → Workflow → Templates → Integration)

  1004→5. Push (`git push origin skill/your-skill-name`)

  1007→### Skill Format Guidelines
  1008→Each skill should include:
  1009→- **Philosophy** — Why this approach, not the conventional one
  1010→- **Workflow** — Phased, actionable steps

  1012→- **Integration** — How it connects to other skills
